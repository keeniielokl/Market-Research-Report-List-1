<p><p>The textile dyestuff market includes various dye types tailored for specific applications. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers, offering bright colors and excellent fastness. Reactive dyes bond chemically with fabric, making them suitable for cellulosic fibers. Sulfur dyes provide dark shades and are often used for denim. Vat dyes require a reduction process for application, ideal for cotton. Acid dyes are typically used for protein fibers and nylon, providing vivid colors and good light fastness.</p></p>
