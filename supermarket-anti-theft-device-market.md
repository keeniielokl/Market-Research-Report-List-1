<p><p>The supermarket anti-theft device market consists of three main types: Radio Frequency (RF) systems, Acousto-Magnetic (AM) systems, and Electromagnetic Wave (EM) systems. RF systems use low-frequency electromagnetic fields to detect tags on merchandise. AM systems, known for their higher sensitivity, utilize magnetic fields and are less prone to interference. EM systems operate with magnetic tape technology, effectively securing high-value items. Each type offers unique advantages in terms of signal strength, range, and susceptibility to interference, catering to various retail needs.</p></p>
