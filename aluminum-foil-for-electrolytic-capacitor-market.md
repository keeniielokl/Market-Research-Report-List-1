<p><p>The aluminum foil market for electrolytic capacitors is divided into two primary types: anode foil and cathode foil. Anode foil is a high-purity aluminum that is etched to increase surface area, enhancing capacitance and performance in capacitors. In contrast, cathode foil, although it can also be etched, primarily serves as a conductive layer to complete the electrochemical cell. Both types are essential for manufacturing efficient capacitors, impacting various applications including electronics and energy storage systems.</p></p>
