<p><p>The pediatric growth hormone market comprises several key types based on underlying conditions. Growth hormone deficiency occurs when the body doesn't produce enough growth hormone, leading to stunted growth. Turner syndrome, a genetic condition affecting girls, leads to short stature due to hormonal imbalances. Prader-Willi syndrome, characterized by obesity and developmental issues, also requires growth hormone treatment. Noonan syndrome, a genetic disorder, may result in growth delays as well. Lastly, the "Others" category includes various rare conditions necessitating growth hormone therapy.</p></p>
