<p><ul><li>Cast Aluminum Cookware</li><li>Anodized Aluminum Cookware</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The aluminum cookware market is primarily categorized into cast aluminum and anodized aluminum cookware. Cast aluminum cookware is made by pouring molten aluminum into molds, offering even heat distribution and durability, often featuring non-stick coatings. Anodized aluminum cookware, on the other hand, undergoes an electrochemical process that strengthens the metal and enhances resistance to corrosion and scratching, providing a non-reactive surface that is easy to clean. Both types cater to different cooking preferences and performance needs in kitchens.</p></p>
