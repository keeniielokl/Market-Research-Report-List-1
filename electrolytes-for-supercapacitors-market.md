<p><p>The electrolytes for supercapacitors are categorized into three types: aqueous, organic, and ionic liquids. Aqueous electrolytes, comprising water-based solutions, offer high conductivity and safety but have limited voltage ranges. Organic electrolytes use organic solvents, enabling higher energy densities and broader voltage windows, but may pose safety concerns. Ionic liquids are non-volatile, thermally stable liquids that provide excellent electrochemical performance and operational stability, though they are often more expensive. Each type balances performance, safety, and cost for energy storage applications.</p></p>
